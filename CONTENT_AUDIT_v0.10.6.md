# ByteCase Playbooks v0.10.6 Content Audit

## Goal

This sprint focused on content credibility. The goal was to tighten wording, reduce mismatched guidance, avoid accidental recommendations, and make training questions test examiner judgment instead of visual pattern recognition.

## Audit checklist

Reviewed areas:

- Artifact questions
- Artifact areas and mapped guidance
- Chained Coaching paths
- Coach Mode drill questions
- Tool/method examples
- Guardrails
- Glossary/search language

## Content rules reinforced

- Use “may support,” “may help evaluate,” and “context” language when a source does not prove a conclusion by itself.
- Separate device activity, account activity, artifact presence, and human actor attribution.
- Do not claim intent, knowledge, concealment, malware, compromise, transfer, or authorship from a single artifact unless explicitly supported.
- Keep tool names as examples of commonly used industry tools or methods, not recommendations or validation statements.
- Tie stronger conclusions to source, scope, tool/version, timestamp meaning, corroboration, and limitations.

## Remaining content work

Future content polish should focus on:

- Expanding the built-in question bank topic-by-topic
- Adding more coaching paths only after the current ones are field-tested
- Reviewing artifact-area tool examples against agency/vendor-neutral wording
- Adding release-candidate proofreading before signing/public release
